Archived so the run can be audited from its exact inputs.

## Property under test

Two independent private transfers hit the SAME shared sender contract. Because
each spend's two nullifiers are its EIP-8250 keyed-nonce set at sequence zero,
the two transfers share the sender's address but occupy disjoint nonce
namespaces. They are therefore both admissible in any order with no
sequential account nonce serializing them. A single shared account nonce would
force the second to seq 1 and reject it at seq 0. That rejection is exactly
what a replay of either mined transfer now produces.

## Replayability

The keyed nonces of both spends are consumed on Hegot√°, so both raws are
permanently rejected at admission (`expected 1, got 0`). The fixture's output
note secrets are deterministic under the generator's seed (`w.set_seed`), so
the 0.475 ETH now held in output notes at leaves 7-10 (0.025 ETH went to the
two paymasters as fees) is reconstructable by re-running the generator.
Reproducing the frame-level race needs a fresh pair of shields; the commands
above are the specification.
